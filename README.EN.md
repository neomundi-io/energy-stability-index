# Energy Stability Index

## Working English Version

Public methodological notes on the NeoMundi Energy Stability Index for AI runtime governance.

This repository documents a public conceptual definition of the ESI.

It does not disclose production coefficients, internal weights, thresholds, optimization parameters, full decision rules, or runtime inference mechanisms used in the NeoMundi engine.

## Definition

The ESI, or Energy Stability Index, is a composite runtime governance index.

It aims to provide a synthetic reading of the state of an AI generation or execution segment based on several observable dimensions.

The ESI may be presented on an operational scale from 0 to 100 in order to facilitate reading, comparison, auditability, and integration into governance systems.

## Conceptual Dimensions

ESI may aggregate several families of signals or metrics, including:

- S: governance stability;
- P: informational density;
- Q: informational energy;
- C: semantic coherence.

ΔG may also contribute to the interpretation of the ESI as a signal of variation, transition, or drift over time.

These dimensions should not be understood as a complete public formula.  
They describe the general conceptual structure of the index.

G belongs to a stability analogy inspired by dynamical systems.

It is not a direct physical measurement, but a normalized signal that helps interpret the governance stability state of an AI generation.

ESI, for its part, does not claim to measure a single physical quantity. It aggregates several observable dimensions in order to produce a synthetic, contextualized, and interpretable reading.

## Role of the ESI

ESI is intended to produce a more global reading than an isolated signal.

It may help observe:

- whether a generation remains governable;
- whether it presents sufficient stability;
- whether it mobilizes high informational effort;
- whether it presents useful or problematic density;
- whether it approaches a transition zone;
- whether it requires an alert, supervision, or a governance decision.

ESI transforms a series of separate signals into a composite reading that is more readable for systems, operators, and audits.

## What the ESI Is Not

ESI is not:

- absolute proof of truth;
- a measure of model consciousness or cognition;
- a guarantee of absence of hallucination;
- an automatic legal certification;
- a universal quality score;
- a moral decision;
- a direct physical measurement of energy;
- an internal reconstruction of the model’s reasoning.

ESI is a runtime governance index, not a universal truth.

## Relationship with Other Signals

ESI does not replace individual signals.

It organizes them into a composite reading.

In this logic:

- G observes a stability state;
- ΔG observes a variation in stability;
- volumetry observes the volumes being handled;
- Dv observes a volumetric transformation;
- E observes an informational load;
- Di observes informational density;
- ESI provides a synthetic governance reading.

The value of the ESI is to enable a multi-signal reading without reducing AI governance to a single metric.

## Operational Use

In a runtime governance system, ESI may contribute to:

- tracking the global state of a generation;
- feeding a dashboard;
- comparing generations or models;
- detecting areas of fragility;
- documenting an audit;
- supporting an ALLOW / FLAG / BLOCK decision;
- providing an actionable indicator to an orchestration system.

ESI should not be used alone to conclude on the truth, compliance, or safety of a generation.

## Methodological Position

ESI is designed as an observability and governance artifact.

It makes it possible to move from a single-signal reading to a multidimensional reading.

Its purpose is not to replace human or domain-specific analysis, but to provide a synthetic, traceable, and interpretable indicator within a given context.

## Limits

The interpretation of ESI depends on the usage context, domain, risk level, configured thresholds, and applied governance rules.

A high score does not guarantee absolute truth.  
A low score does not automatically prove an error.

ESI should be read as a composite signal that supports governance, never as a standalone certification.

Coefficients, thresholds, weights, integration rules, and production parameters belong to the proprietary NeoMundi implementation and are not documented in this public repository.
